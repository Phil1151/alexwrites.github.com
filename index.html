This Weeks Wierd Mythical Creature

This weeks weird mythical creature.... lubber

A lubber fiend is typically described as a large, hairy man with a tail. Sometimes they are
described like a Rusty (or a House Elf if you prefer) who performs housework in
exchange for a saucer of milk and a place in front of the fire.
Or a giant son of a witch and the Devil. This version of a Lubber haunts the wine cellars
and kitchens of abbeys, tempting the monks into drunkenness, gluttony or making sin.

Fate

Collect the threads as they are
cut by fates
Ones that once ran with explosive love
and passion hate.

Stories that made
the thread its own new colour
mainly ones that tinted your
own shade.

You cannot help but observe
that the thread does not speak.
Does not echo the void you miss

Not spring and nut itself
into a form you want.

Sit there bare and soundless
Snapped
On the cold bare lonely earth

You weave it through your fingers and hands
and feel the fibbers tecktores
accidentally wishing that it holds you back
in reassurance and love

Just a lifeless piece thread
that once was but now gone

You miss when you think
of a time when you
and them were a pluck in the same
scale on a golden harp

As fate delclared it time for you to meet
Your songs for that time were unique
barely recorded and never be replicated

The silence is now
present within the spaces
of the songs you now
relive
However
still collect the thread
evan thou snapped and gone
and knit it into a scarf
and wair it snug and proud around your neck
warm
till you feel ok

The Moth Effect

Pleasant are the times when you try to fish for Moths,
with a light bulb dangling on a fishing rod,
with its power being run straight from your heart,
as it dangles over the cliff edge during a lightning storm.

And only 1 comes from the dark,
it flaps madly, its wings sound like flames
battling to stay alive after a puff of the lips.

Effected by its
delicate flawed architecture
but also by your power, pride
and self perspective.

Shall you crack it
between the point of your finger
and the solid plaster wall.
You'll hear nothing of the consequences
as the moth effect is silent
